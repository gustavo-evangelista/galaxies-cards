The largest galaxies

And curiosities about them

IC 1101

IC 1101 is the largest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical to lenticular and appears golden yellow in color. The brightest galaxy is located over 1.04 billion light-years from Earth.

Phoenix Cluster

The Phoenix Cluster is one of the most massive groups of galaxies in the universe. It is located approximately 5.7 billion light-years from Earth. The elliptical galaxy is composed of medium and dark intracluster matter.

NGC 262

NGC 262, located 202 million light-years from Earth, is one of the largest spiral galaxies known. The galaxy's diameter is approximately 1.3 million light-years.

NGC 4889

NGC 4889, also known as "Coma B," is a supergiant elliptical galaxy located approximately 308 million light-years from Earth. It was also discovered by British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.

NGC 4874

NGC 4874, also known as "Coma A," was discovered in 1785 by British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I. It is located 350 million light-years from Earth. It is a smooth, ball-shaped galaxy surrounded by a stellar circle.

A2261-BCG

A2261-BCG is located approximately 3 billion light-years from Earth. It is elliptical in shape and was discovered by the Hubble Space Telescope. A2261-BCG is one million light-years across.
